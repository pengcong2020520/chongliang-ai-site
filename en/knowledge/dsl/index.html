DEFINED TERM

Why DSL Matters in the AI Era

A DSL is a language designed for a specific domain. Its value is to compress complex tasks into stable expressions shared by people, models, and systems.

Definition: A DSL, or domain-specific language, is an expression system designed for a particular problem domain. In AI workflows, it can become the intermediate layer between human intent, model reasoning, and system execution.

Why AI makes DSLs more important

Language models are good at natural language, but natural language can be ambiguous. Systems need clearer constraints, structure, and boundaries. A DSL provides a more stable layer between natural language and executable interfaces.

It does not replace natural language

A good DSL does not have to be complex or programming-language-like. It can be structured fields, an annotation standard, a task protocol, or an intermediate format that makes model output more stable.
